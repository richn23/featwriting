# FEAT AI
## Are you AI ready?

**Positioning:** FEAT AI is a performance-based assessment that measures whether someone is genuinely ready to work with AI — across awareness, capability, responsibility, and critical judgement.

---

### The problem

The market is flooded with AI readiness courses — LinkedIn Learning, Google AI Essentials, Coursera, corporate L&D, university modules. What's missing is a credible way to prove who actually came out the other side ready.

Employers rolling out AI tools, universities running AI literacy modules, and individuals building their CVs all need the same thing: evidence of capability, not just a certificate of attendance.

---

### What FEAT AI measures

Four dimensions, each scored on the standard FEAT 4-tier scale (Not Yet Competent → Competent → Merit → Distinction):

- **Aware** — understands what AI is, what it can and can't do, where the risks sit
- **Capable** — can use AI tools effectively for real tasks, including prompting, evaluating outputs, and integrating them into workflows
- **Responsible** — knows when to trust AI, when to verify, and the ethical, privacy, and bias considerations
- **Critical** — can judge output quality, spot hallucinations, and recognise when human judgement is needed

The result is a profile that shows what the person can actually do — not a single pass/fail score.

---

### Who it's for

FEAT AI is deliberately cross-domain. The same assessment engine serves:

- **Universities** running AI literacy modules and pre-sessional programmes
- **Employers** rolling out AI tools who need to know which staff are ready
- **Government and workforce programmes** measuring AI skills uplift at scale
- **Individuals** wanting a credential that proves capability, not attendance

---

### Why FEAT, not a quiz

Most AI assessments rely on multiple-choice questions about how AI works. FEAT AI uses authentic, scenario-based tasks that simulate real situations. You can't fake your way through them, and every task is self-contained — no reward for prior topic knowledge.

We don't teach. We measure. That's the difference between a course completion certificate and an evidence-based credential.

---

### How it works

A FEAT AI assessment is a short series of self-contained tasks. Each task presents a realistic scenario — a workplace problem, an academic question, a decision to make — and asks the candidate to use AI to address it. The system evaluates the response across the four dimensions, producing a clear capability profile.

---

### What it's anchored in

FEAT AI aligns with global AI competency frameworks, including UNESCO (2024), EU DigComp 2.2, and OECD AI skills work.

These frameworks define what AI competence is. FEAT AI measures whether it is actually demonstrated.

---

**One-line summary:** Courses aim to make people AI-ready. FEAT AI proves whether they are.
